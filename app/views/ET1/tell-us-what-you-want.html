Compensation - what can a tribunal award?
If a tribunal decides you’ve been unfairly dismissed, you’ll normally receive compensation which is made up of:

• a basic award - fixed sum worked out using a standard formula
• a compensatory award - compensation for the actual money you lost due to your dismissal

Compensation can be reduced for various reasons and a tribunal will tell you more about this if it applies in your specific circumstances.

In disciplinary or grievance cases, you may be entitled to an increased award if an employer fails to follow the guidance set out in the Acas Code of Practice on disciplinary and grievance procedures.

Similarly, awards can be reduced if a tribunal decides you’ve failed to follow the guidance set out in the Acas Code of Practice on disciplinary and grievance procedures.

In certain claims, such as those involving discrimination, a tribunal may compensate you for ‘injury to feelings’ (individual hurt and distress you may have suffered) in addition to compensation for financial loss.
What is a tribunal recommendation?
Depending on the nature and details of your claim, a tribunal can make a variety of recommendations and in the past included recommending:

• employers review and update their equality and diversity policies
• employees responsible for discrimination attend diversity and equality training
• employers put a copy of a tribunal’s decision on their company noticeboard to make it clear to all employees a tribunal has decided discrimination has occurred

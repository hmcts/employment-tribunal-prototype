What type of discrimination are you claiming?

What is discrimination?

Discrimination is when you’re unfairly treated because of any protected characteristics from the 2010 Equality Act. You have rights at work if you’ve experienced:

  • direct discrimination
  • indirect discrimination
  • harassment
  • victimisation

See the GOV.UK guidance (opens in new tab) for further information on protected characteristics and your workplace rights.

Do you have an ‘Acas early conciliation certificate’ for the respondent or respondents you’re claiming against?

To make a claim you usually need to get an Acas early conciliation certificate (or give a valid reason why you do not have one) for each respondent you’re making your claim against.

Acas will have sent you certificates after you initially contacted them to try and settle your dispute through early conciliation.

The certificates include the details of the respondent you’re making your claim against along with a number to identify them. You’ll need to provide these later as part of your claim.

If you’re claiming against multiple respondents after 1 December 2021, your certificate numbers should exist on one notification letter.
